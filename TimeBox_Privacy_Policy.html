Privacy Summary: TimeBox is designed with privacy at its core. All your data stays on your device. We don't collect, store, or share your personal information with any third parties. No servers, no cloud sync, no tracking.

1. Introduction

Welcome to TimeBox ("we," "our," or "us"). This Privacy Policy explains how the TimeBox mobile application ("App") collects, uses, and protects your information. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring you have a positive experience using our App.

TimeBox is developed and operated by Adam Markocki ("Developer"). By using TimeBox, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Data Collected and Stored Locally

TimeBox stores the following data locally on your device only:

Data Type Description Storage Method
Username Optional display name you provide UserDefaults (local)
Tasks & Time Blocks Your daily tasks, schedules, and time blocks JSON files (local)
Brain Dump Entries Notes and thoughts you capture JSON files (local)
App Preferences Theme (Light/Dark/System), Language (English/Polish) UserDefaults (local)
Schedule Settings Day start/end times, default time block duration UserDefaults (local)
Notification Preferences Whether notifications are enabled UserDefaults (local)
Onboarding Status Whether you've completed initial setup UserDefaults (local)

Important: All data listed above is stored exclusively on your device. It is never transmitted to external servers, cloud services, or third parties.

2.2 Data We Do NOT Collect

TimeBox explicitly does NOT collect, access, or transmit:

  • ❌ Personal identification information (beyond optional username)
  • ❌ Email addresses or phone numbers
  • ❌ Location data (GPS or otherwise)
  • ❌ Contacts
  • ❌ Photos or camera access
  • ❌ Microphone or audio data
  • ❌ Health or fitness data
  • ❌ Usage analytics or telemetry
  • ❌ Device identifiers (IDFA, UUID)
  • ❌ Browsing history
  • ❌ Crash reports
  • ❌ Any form of tracking data

2.3 Purchase Information

If you choose to support development through the in-app Tip Jar (voluntary donations), purchase transactions are processed entirely by Apple through StoreKit. We do not collect, store, or have access to your payment information. Apple may collect transaction data according to their Privacy Policy.

3. How We Use Your Information

The data stored locally on your device is used solely for the following purposes:

3.1 App Functionality

  • Task Management: To display and manage your daily tasks, schedules, and time blocks
  • Brain Dump: To store and retrieve your notes and thoughts
  • Personalization: To remember your preferences (theme, language, working hours)
  • Data Persistence: To save your data between app sessions

3.2 Notifications

  • Task Reminders: To send local notifications for scheduled time blocks (only if you grant notification permission)
  • Support Reminders: Optional weekly notification to encourage app support (can be disabled by turning off notifications)

All notifications are generated and delivered locally by your device using Apple's UserNotifications framework. No data is sent to external servers.

3.3 In-App Purchases

  • Tip Jar: To process voluntary donations through Apple's StoreKit framework
  • No Feature Unlocking: Tips are purely voluntary and do not unlock any app features

4. Data Sharing and Third Parties

4.1 No Data Sharing

We do not share, sell, rent, or trade your data with anyone. Your data never leaves your device.

4.2 Third-Party Services

TimeBox uses only the following Apple frameworks:

Service Purpose Data Access
StoreKit In-app purchases (Tip Jar) Apple manages payment data
UserNotifications Local notifications Handled locally on your device
SwiftUI/UIKit User interface No data transmitted
Foundation Data storage (UserDefaults, FileManager) Stored locally on your device

No Third-Party SDKs: TimeBox does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or tracking services.

4.3 Apple's Role

While we don't collect data, Apple may collect certain information when you:

  • Download the app from the App Store
  • Make in-app purchases
  • Use Apple's system services

Apple's data practices are governed by the Apple Privacy Policy.

5. Data Storage and Security

5.1 Local Storage

All your data is stored locally on your device using:

  • UserDefaults: For app preferences and settings
  • File System (JSON files): For tasks, schedules, and brain dump entries

This data is protected by your device's operating system security measures, including:

  • iOS sandbox security (app data is isolated)
  • Device encryption (if enabled on your device)
  • Passcode/biometric protection (Face ID/Touch ID)

5.2 Data Backup

Your TimeBox data may be included in:

  • iCloud Backup: If you have iCloud backup enabled, your data is backed up encrypted to your personal iCloud account
  • iTunes/Finder Backup: If you back up your device to a computer

These backups are controlled by you and managed by Apple according to their privacy policies.

5.3 Security Measures

We implement the following security practices:

  • All data stored in iOS app sandbox (isolated from other apps)
  • No network connections for data transmission
  • No external servers or databases
  • Regular code reviews for security best practices
  • Use of Apple's secure frameworks only

6. Data Retention and Deletion

6.1 Data Retention

Your data is retained locally on your device for as long as:

  • You keep the app installed
  • You don't manually delete data
  • You don't restore your device

6.2 Data Deletion

You can delete your data at any time by:

  1. Deleting Individual Items: Delete specific tasks, time blocks, or brain dump entries within the app
  2. Clearing App Data: Use DataManager.clearAllData() to delete all daily plans
  3. Resetting Settings: Reset app preferences in Settings
  4. Uninstalling the App: Deleting TimeBox from your device permanently removes all local data

Note: Deleting the app permanently removes all data. This action cannot be undone unless you have an iCloud or iTunes backup.

7. Your Privacy Rights

7.1 General Rights

  • Right to Modify: Edit tasks, schedules, and settings at will
  • Right to Delete: Delete individual items or all data

7.2 European Union (GDPR) Rights

If you are in the European Union or European Economic Area, you have additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Right to be Informed: This privacy policy provides transparency
  • Right to Access: Access your data within the app
  • Right to Rectification: Correct any inaccurate data

  • Right to Restrict Processing: Stop using the app
  • Right to Data Portability: Export data via device backup
  • Right to Object: Disable specific features (e.g., notifications)

Legal Basis for Processing: Consent and legitimate interests (app functionality).

7.3 California (CCPA/CPRA) Rights

If you are a California resident, you have rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

  • Right to Know: This policy discloses what data we collect
  • Right to Delete: Delete data by uninstalling the app
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale: We do not sell your data
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We don't discriminate based on privacy rights exercise

Note: Since we don't collect or transmit data, most CCPA rights are inherently satisfied.
